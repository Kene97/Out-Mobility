# Go-to-Market — Out-door

## GTM Model

**Motion:** B2B2C — sell to fleet operators, monetize through advertisers.

The fleet operator is the distribution channel. The advertiser is the revenue source.
We serve both, but we acquire through operators first.

---

## Launch Market

**Target city:** One high-density urban market with a significant ride-hail
and taxi ecosystem.

**Selection criteria:**
- High trip volume (proof of attention scale)
- Existing ride-hail operator relationships
- Advertiser demand for urban audiences
- Regulatory clarity on in-vehicle media

**Launch posture:** Controlled. Deploy 50–100 vehicles. Prove the model. Then scale.

---

## Phase 1: Operator Acquisition

**Goal:** Sign 3–5 fleet operators or aggregators covering 50–200 vehicles.

**Offer to operators:**
- Free hardware installation (or revenue-share offset)
- Passive monthly revenue from ad network
- Fleet management dashboard (added operational value)
- No upfront cost. No risk.

**Sales motion:** Founder-led. Direct outreach. 1:1 deals.

**Key message to operators:** "We put a screen in your vehicle. You earn every time
someone sees an ad. We manage everything."

---

## Phase 2: Advertiser Acquisition

**Goal:** Sign 3–5 paying advertisers for the launch cohort.

**Offer to advertisers:**
- Verified impressions (trip-level data)
- Targeted by route, time, vehicle type
- Real-time campaign dashboard
- CPM-based pricing with minimum spend floors

**Sales motion:** Founder-led. Target brand managers and media agencies.

**Key message to advertisers:** "We sell verified attention inside vehicles.
You know exactly how many people saw your ad, for how long, and where."

---

## Pricing Model

### Advertiser Side
- **CPM pricing:** Cost per 1,000 verified impressions
- **Minimum campaign:** $500–$2,000 depending on market
- **Premium inventory:** Route-targeted, time-targeted campaigns at premium CPM

### Operator Side
- **Revenue share:** 30–40% of net ad revenue per vehicle per month
- **No upfront cost** to operator in Phase 1
- Hardware cost recovered through revenue share over 6–12 months

---

## Growth Levers

1. **Vehicle count** — more vehicles = more impressions = more advertiser capacity
2. **Trip density** — high-frequency operators deliver faster ROI
3. **Advertiser repeat rate** — campaign renewal is the primary retention metric
4. **Data quality** — better impression data commands higher CPM over time
