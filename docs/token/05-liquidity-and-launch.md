# Liquidity & Launch Readiness

## Current Status

**$OUT has not launched.**

There is no token in circulation. There is no exchange listing. There is no
trading pair. Anyone claiming otherwise is not affiliated with Out Mobility.

This page exists to be fully transparent about where the token stands,
what the launch plan looks like, and what to expect — and when.

---

## Why Not Launched Yet?

$OUT will launch when Out Mobility is ready — not before.

A token launch before the network has real activity is a speculation event,
not an ecosystem event. We are not building a speculation token. We are building
utility infrastructure. Launching the token before the infrastructure exists
would be the wrong order of operations.

The sequence is:

1. Build and launch Out-door (in-car advertising) — **Active**
2. Grow a real advertiser and operator base — **In progress**
3. Establish verified network activity and revenue — **Upcoming**
4. Launch $OUT into a network that already has real demand — **Planned**

This is the responsible path. It is also the path most likely to produce a
token that holds value over time.

## Liquidity Design

10% of total supply (100,000,000 $OUT) is reserved specifically for liquidity
provision. This allocation exists to ensure:

- Traders can buy and sell $OUT without excessive price impact
- The market has depth from day one
- Liquidity is not entirely dependent on retail participation

The liquidity allocation will be deployed at TGE, managed by the Out Mobility
team and any appointed liquidity partners.

Details on liquidity venues and market-making arrangements will be published
prior to TGE.

## What "Coming Soon" Actually Means

Out Mobility will not give a specific launch date until we are confident
the date is real.

Premature date announcements in the crypto space are one of the most common
sources of community distrust. We will not do that.

What we will do:
- Keep this documentation updated as plans evolve
- Post meaningful milestones on @OutMobility
- Give the waitlist community advance notice before any public launch event

When we say "coming soon," we mean: the work is happening and we will tell
you when we are ready. That is the only promise we will make.
