# Token Utility

## What Does $OUTSIDE Actually Do?

A token without utility is just a number. $OUTSIDE is designed from the ground up
to serve the functioning of a real network.

Here is how $OUTSIDE works across the Out Mobility ecosystem — both today in its
design intent, and in its planned evolution.

---

## Core Utility Functions

### 1. Advertising Payments (Out-door)

Advertisers who run campaigns on the Out-door network can pay for inventory
using $OUTSIDE.

This creates direct, recurring demand for the token from a growing base of
businesses that need to reach audiences inside vehicles. As the advertiser
network grows, so does the baseline demand for $OUTSIDE.

### 2. Driver & Fleet Operator Rewards

Drivers and fleet operators are the backbone of the Out Mobility network.
They provide the vehicles. They carry the screens. They generate the impressions.

$OUTSIDE is the mechanism through which their contribution is recognised and
rewarded. As the network grows, drivers and operators earn $OUTSIDE for verified
trips, uptime, and ad delivery performance.

This is not a points programme. These are real tokens earned through real
contribution to a real network.

### 3. Staking for Premium Access

Advertisers who hold and stake $OUTSIDE can unlock premium campaign features:
priority placement, advanced targeting, early access to new inventory, and
enhanced reporting.

Staking creates a reason to hold $OUTSIDE beyond speculation — and it aligns
advertiser behaviour with the long-term health of the network.

### 4. Ecosystem Settlement

As Out Mobility launches Out-side (ride-hailing), Out-leaf (carbon), and
Out-charge (EV charging), $OUTSIDE becomes the common settlement layer across
all four products.

- A rider completes a trip on Out-side → earns $OUTSIDE back
- A carbon credit is issued through Out-leaf → denominated in $OUTSIDE
- A vehicle charges through Out-charge → settled partially in $OUTSIDE

The token flows through the entire stack — not just one product.

### 5. Governance Participation

Token holders will have the ability to participate in governance decisions
about the Out Mobility ecosystem.

This is not a promise of control. It is a commitment to community alignment.
Decisions about ecosystem parameters, allocation proposals, and product
priorities will be put to token holders as the network matures.

The degree and structure of governance will be defined before launch and
clearly communicated to the community.

---

## How $OUTSIDE Supports the Ecosystem

The token does three things for the ecosystem at a structural level:

**It creates internal demand.**
Advertising payments, staking requirements, and cross-product settlement all
require $OUTSIDE. This is not artificial. This is the token doing its job.

**It aligns incentives.**
Drivers, operators, advertisers, and riders all earn, spend, or hold $OUTSIDE.
Everyone has a reason to want the network to succeed.

**It makes the ecosystem composable.**
A token that flows freely between Out-door, Out-side, Out-leaf, and Out-charge
means value created in one part of the network is accessible across all parts.
That is a fundamentally different model from a siloed points system.

---

## Connecting to Future Products

The Out Mobility roadmap extends beyond the four current products.

Future utility areas being explored include:

- **Data marketplace** — access to verified mobility and audience data,
  purchased using $OUTSIDE
- **DePIN expansion** — $OUTSIDE as a reward token for physical infrastructure
  contributors across new markets and geographies
- **Partner integrations** — third-party platforms that accept or integrate
  $OUTSIDE within the broader mobility economy

These are future-looking intentions, not confirmed features. They will be
announced when they are ready to build.

---

## Utility Is Earned, Not Assumed

$OUTSIDE utility grows as the network grows.

In the early stages, utility is focused on Out-door: advertising payments
and driver rewards. As Out-side, Out-leaf, and Out-charge launch, utility
expands naturally.

This is the correct order. A token should earn its utility by powering real
transactions — not by promising utility that does not yet exist.
