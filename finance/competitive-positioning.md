## Direct Competitors

### 1. Traditional DOOH (Billboard Networks)

Companies: Posterscope Nigeria, Kinetic, local billboard operators.

| Dimension | Traditional DOOH | Out-door |
|---|---|---|
| Impressions | Estimated (panel survey) | Verified (trip telemetry) |
| Targeting | Fixed location only | Geo + time + route |
| Campaign management | Manual (agency-mediated) | Self-serve dashboard |
| Audience | Passerby (glancing) | Captive (20+ min dwell) |
| Data | None post-campaign | Real-time delivery data |
| CPM | $4–8 | $6–12 |
| Min spend | $5,000+ | $500 |

**Our advantage:** A brand manager who has seen a verified impression report from Out-door
will never go back to estimated billboard metrics. The trust gap is permanent.

**Their advantage:** Established relationships with media buyers.
Scale and reach they've built over decades.

**Our strategy:** Target brands already spending on DOOH who want accountability.
Position Out-door as the "DOOH 2.0" with verification.

---

### 2. In-App Ride-Hailing Ads (Uber Ads, Bolt)

Companies: Uber Advertising (in-app), Bolt Ads (in-app).

| Dimension | Uber/Bolt In-App Ads | Out-door |
|---|---|---|
| Surface | Passenger's phone screen | Dedicated tablet in vehicle |
| Attention | Split (user's phone + app) | Full-screen, no competing content |
| Verification | Click-through / impression | Trip telemetry + heartbeat |
| Creative formats | Banner, sponsored listing | Full video, full screen image |
| CPM | $8–15 | $6–12 |
| Availability | Uber/Bolt users only | Any vehicle on our network |
| Data | User behavioral | Location + time |
| Africa availability | Limited | Built for Africa |

**Our advantage:** The screen belongs entirely to us. Uber in-app ads are competing
with maps, trip status, and the passenger's own phone for attention.
Our screen is the only thing in the sightline.

**Their advantage:** More precise user data (Uber knows who the passenger is).
Larger existing advertiser relationships.

**Our strategy:** We are not trying to replace Uber Ads.
We are the product for brands who want the vehicle as a media surface, not the app.

### 3. In-Vehicle Screen Networks (Existing Players)

Companies: Alfi (US, taxis), Vugo (US, ride-hail), Autoscreen (AU).

These are the closest category comparables. All Western markets.

| Dimension | Alfi / Vugo (US) | Out-door |
|---|---|---|
| Market | US taxis / ride-hail | Nigeria / West Africa |
| Verification | GPS + impression log | Same + heartbeat correlation |
| Device model | Company-owned tablets | Operator-owned (asset-light) |
| Business model | Revenue share | Same |
| Data | US-scale | Building Africa-scale |
| Status | Alfi publicly traded, struggling | We are pre-public, focused |

**Key insight from Alfi's public data:** Even in the US, in-vehicle ads are undermonetized
because advertisers don't trust the impressions. Our verification-first approach
solves the problem that Alfi has not fully solved.

**Our advantage:** We are building in an untouched market with no incumbent.
In the US, Alfi fights for the same vehicles as Uber Ads. In Lagos, we are alone.

### 4. Indoor DOOH (Mall Screens, Airport Screens)

Companies: Viacom Outdoor Nigeria, local mall screen operators.

| Dimension | Indoor DOOH | Out-door |
|---|---|---|
| Audience | Shoppers, travelers | Commuters, professionals |
| Dwell time | 3–8 min per screen | 20–40 min per trip |
| Verification | Footfall estimates | GPS + trip duration |
| Reach | Fixed to location | Mobile — follows the city |
| Premium context | High (malls, airports) | Depends on fleet quality |
| CPM | $6–15 | $6–12 |

**Our advantage:** Mobile reach. An airport screen reaches 10,000 travelers/day
in one location. A fleet of 5,000 vehicles reaches 50,000+ passengers/day
across every corner of Lagos.

**Their advantage:** Premium context (malls, airports signal wealth).
No competition on content (no other screens nearby).

## Why Our Unit Economics Improve With Scale (Structural Advantage)

This is the key insight competitors cannot replicate by writing a check:

```
Scale → more devices → more impressions available →
→ more advertiser demand → better fill rate →
→ higher CPM (scarcity pricing) →
→ better economics per device →
→ better operator revenue share →
→ more operators join →
→ more devices → [repeat]
```

**Competitor response:** Build their own network.
- Cost: $600K+ in device subsidy + 18 months of field operations
- By the time they reach 500 devices, we're at 5,000
- Our data advantage widens every day we operate

This is why the window is NOW. The infrastructure race has started.
The first company to lock in 10,000+ devices wins the category.
