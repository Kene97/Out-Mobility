# Go-to-Market Strategy — Out-door

**This is not a marketing plan. It is a sequenced market entry playbook.**
Every step has a clear trigger before the next step starts.

---

## GTM Philosophy

We are not selling advertising. We are signing infrastructure contracts.

**Demand side (advertisers):** Don't chase small budgets. Sign 2–3 anchor brands
who can prove the category to peers. Let them become references.

**Supply side (operators):** Don't chase individual drivers. Sign fleet operators.
One operator with 100 vehicles is worth 100 individual driver conversations.

**Sequence matters:** Supply before demand.
You cannot sell campaigns to advertisers without fleet. Don't reverse this.

---

## Phase 0: Pre-Launch (Months 1–3)

**Goal:** Sign 2 operators and 1 advertiser before first device goes live.

### Supply First: Sign First Operators (Month 1–2)

**Target:** 2 fleet operators covering 50–100 vehicles combined.

**Target operator profile:**
- Organized fleet (not individual drivers)
- Ride-hail affiliated (Uber/Bolt registered) or corporate transport
- Operator is accessible (known relationship or warm intro)
- Fleet is relatively modern (2019+ vehicles)

**Operator outreach approach:**

Step 1: Map the landscape
- List all major ride-hail fleet operators in Lagos
- Identify transportation companies with managed fleets
- Corporate fleet managers (banks, telcos, logistics companies)

Step 2: Warm intro first
- Use personal network to get introductions to fleet managers
- Attend ride-hail driver events, fleet operator WhatsApp groups
- Contact Uber/Bolt's driver operations team for fleet partner list

Step 3: The pitch (operator)
```
"We want to put screens in your vehicles.
We install them, we manage them, we fill them with ads.
You get 35% of revenue every month — no work required.
Your driver keeps the car. You earn the extra income.
First 10 vehicles: zero cost. We prove it works. You see the check."
```

Step 4: Pilot agreement
- Simple 1-page MOU (memorandum of understanding)
- Revenue share terms
- Device responsibility (they handle physical damage)
- Exit clause: either party can exit with 30 days notice

**Target: 2 signed operators, 50+ vehicles committed, by end of Month 2.**

### Demand Second: Sign First Advertiser (Month 2–3)

**Target:** 1 brand that will run a paid campaign the moment devices go live.
Not a letter of intent. A purchase order.

**Target advertiser profile:**
- Consumer brand with advertising budget
- Category: FMCG, fintech, telecom, food & beverage
- Decision maker: CMO, Head of Marketing, or Brand Manager
- Has existing DOOH or digital spend (so they understand the category)
- Can sign fast (no 90-day procurement process)

**Outreach approach:**

Step 1: Identify target brands
- FMCG: Indomie, Dangote, Nigerian Breweries, PZ Cussons
- Fintech: PiggyVest, Moniepoint, Kuda Bank, Palmpay
- Telecom: MTN, Airtel (their agencies — not direct)
- Food: Chicken Republic, Tastee Fried Chicken, local QSR chains

Step 2: Go through agencies (not brand direct)
- Nigerian ad agencies control the budget allocation
- Target: Insight BBDO, Noah's Ark, X3M Ideas, DDB Lagos
- These agencies have clients spending on DOOH — pitch the upgrade

Step 3: The pitch (advertiser)
```
"We are launching a verified in-car advertising network in Lagos.
You'll be our launch advertiser. Your campaign runs across 50 vehicles.
We will send you a verified impression report — GPS location, timestamp,
device ID — proving every impression was delivered.
Launch rate: $6 CPM. Minimum: $1,000.
You're not buying estimated eyeballs. You're buying proof."
```

Step 4: Pilot campaign structure
- Budget: $1,000–$3,000 (low enough to approve quickly)
- Duration: 2 weeks (short enough to see results fast)
- Output: Branded verified impression report (investment-grade data)

**Target: 1 committed advertiser with PO or payment before devices go live.**
This is the proof that demand exists before we deploy supply.

## Phase 1: Pilot Launch (Months 3–6)

**Goal:** 50 active devices, 3 advertisers, $2,000+ MRR, verified impression report delivered.

### Device Deployment Playbook

```
Week 1: Receive tablets, flash firmware, enroll in MDM
Week 2: Coordinate with operators for vehicle selection
Week 3: Install tablets in 25 vehicles (target 1 operator)
Week 4: Go live, first campaign running
Week 5: Deliver first verified impression report to advertiser
Week 6: Second operator, 25 more vehicles
Week 7–8: Refine process, prepare for scale
```

**Installation logistics:**
- 2 hours per vehicle (mount, power, configure, test)
- Do 5 vehicles per day with 2 people = 25 vehicles in 5 days
- Founder present for first 20 installations (learn what breaks)

### Advertiser Acquisition at Pilot Stage

**After first campaign delivers:**
1. Request a testimonial from the advertiser (key reference)
2. Schedule a "campaign review" call — show them the data
3. Ask for renewal AND for an introduction to 2 other brands
4. Use the verified impression report as marketing collateral

**Sales pitch after first success:**
"[Brand X] ran 2 weeks, $1,500 budget, received a verified impression report.
Here is their data. Here is their reaction. Do you want the same?"

**Target: 3 paying advertisers by Month 6.**

## Phase 2: Scale (Months 7–18)

**Goal:** 1,000 devices, 10+ advertisers, $9,000+ MRR, repeatable process.

### Supply Scale: From 50 to 1,000 Devices

**Operator acquisition flywheel:**

```
First operator earns ₦50,000/month from 50 devices
  → Other fleet operators see this
    → Warm introductions multiply
      → Each new operator reduces marginal acquisition time
        → By Month 12: operators approach us
```

**Targeted operator segments:**

| Segment | Vehicle Count | Approach | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ride-hail aggregators | 50–500 | Direct partnership | High |
| Corporate fleets | 10–50 | HR/Admin decision maker | Medium |
| Transport cooperatives | 50–200 | Co-op leadership | High |
| Individual fleet owners | 5–20 | WhatsApp groups, events | Low (too slow) |

**Rule:** Never do 1-by-1 driver acquisition. Always through fleet operators.

### Demand Scale: From 3 to 20 Advertisers

**Sales motion at Scale stage:**

| Channel | Target | Metric |
|---|---|---|
| Agency relationships | Lagos top 10 ad agencies | 2 campaigns per agency/quarter |
| Direct enterprise | CMOs at top 50 Nigerian brands | 5 enterprise accounts by Month 12 |
| SME self-serve (future) | Small local brands | Launch basic self-serve in Month 15 |
| Events | Marketing conferences, pitch days | 2 events/quarter |

**Account management:**
- Every advertiser gets a "Campaign Manager" (founder initially)
- Weekly impression update email (automated from dashboard)
- Monthly campaign review call
- Proactive renewal conversation 2 weeks before campaign ends

### Pricing at Scale

When we hit 500 devices and $50K/month in gross impression volume:
- Raise standard CPM to $8.00
- Introduce Premium tier at $10.00 (geo-targeted)
- Announce pricing increase 60 days in advance to existing advertisers
- Lock current advertisers at old rate for 90 days (goodwill + retention)

## Phase 3: City Expansion (Months 18–36)

**Goal:** 4 Nigerian cities, 5,000 devices, $90,000+ MRR.

### City 2: Abuja (Month 16–18)

**Why Abuja second:**
- Federal capital — government, embassies, corporate HQ
- Ride-hail penetration highest after Lagos
- Premium audience (higher-income commuters)
- Advertisers with Lagos campaigns want national reach

**Abuja go-to-market:**
- Lagos playbook, repeated
- Hire 1 Abuja city manager (local)
- Sign 1 anchor operator (government transport, corporate fleet)
- Use Lagos advertiser relationships for cross-city packages

**Pricing premium in Abuja:** +$1.00–1.50 CPM (premium audience premium).

### Cities 3–4: Kano + Port Harcourt (Month 24–30)

**Why:**
- Kano: largest city in northern Nigeria, massive commercial fleet
- Port Harcourt: oil industry wealth, premium vehicle density
- These complete "national reach" for Lagos-headquartered advertisers

**Unlock national CPM premium:** Advertisers running in all 4 cities pay 15% premium for "National Network" package.

## The "Liquidity Threshold" Insight

At a certain fleet density, the economics become self-reinforcing:

**Below liquidity threshold (<100 devices):**
- Advertisers hesitant (not enough reach)
- Operators uncertain (not enough social proof)
- Founder sells everything manually
- Fill rate low (not enough demand)

**At liquidity threshold (~200–300 devices):**
- Advertisers can get meaningful reach
- Operators hear from peers that it works
- Second and third campaigns become easy sells
- Fill rate reaches 50%+ (sustainable economics)

**Conclusion: Raise enough to push through the liquidity threshold in 90 days.**
Pre-seed should deploy 50 devices quickly. Seed should reach 300+ within 90 days of close.
Don't drip-deploy. Hit the threshold fast.

## What We Are NOT Doing in GTM

| What | Why Not |
|---|---|
| Individual driver acquisition | Too slow. No fleet, no scale. |
| Self-serve launch in Month 1 | Advertisers need hand-holding to trust the product first |
| SME-first targeting | Small budgets + high friction = poor LTV |
| Multi-city simultaneous launch | Network dilution, operational overload |
| Performance marketing for advertisers | They need to see the product, not click an ad |
| Programmatic launch in Year 1 | Complexity before trust is built kills deals |
