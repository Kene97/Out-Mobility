# Revenue Model — Out-door / Out Mobility

**Three scenarios: Conservative, Moderate, Aggressive.**
All grounded in the same unit economics. Difference is execution speed.

---

## Revenue Streams

### Primary (MVP)
1. **Ad impression revenue** — 65% of CPM × verified impressions delivered
2. **Campaign management fee** — $0 for MVP (bundled into CPM margin)

### Secondary (Phase 2+)
3. **Audience data insights** — aggregate location + behavioral data sold to brands ($500–2,000/report)
4. **Premium placement fees** — geo-targeted, time-targeted slot premiums
5. **Operator SaaS fee** — fleet management dashboard subscription ($5–15/vehicle/month)

### Future (Phase 3+)
6. **Platform API access** — programmatic buying API for agency desks
7. **Data licensing** — movement + attention data licensed to market researchers
8. **Out-side revenue** — ride-hailing commission (separate product)

**MVP focus: Stream 1 only.** Everything else is optionality.

## Revenue Quality Metrics

| Metric | Target (Year 2) | Why it matters to investors |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue per device | $19/month | Shows CPM and fill rate health |
| Gross margin | >90% | Software margin — highly scalable |
| Net revenue retention | >120% | Advertisers spend more over time |
| ARR growth rate | >200% YoY | Venture-scale growth |
| Revenue concentration | Top customer <30% of ARR | Diversification health |

**Net Revenue Retention >120%:** Existing advertisers from prior year spend 20% more year-over-year due to increased campaign sizes and fleet coverage expansion. This is the compounding revenue story.
