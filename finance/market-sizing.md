# Market Size Analysis — Out-door / Out Mobility

---

## Why This Category Exists Now

Three forces converged to create this market in 2024–2026:

**1. Smartphone-first advertising saturated the feed.**
CPMs on Meta, Google, and TikTok have tripled since 2020.
Advertisers are desperate for verified, high-attention alternatives.
The feed scroll is the cheapest and most cluttered attention surface on earth.

**2. Ride-hailing created a new captive audience at scale.**
In 2016, Lagos had ~5,000 Uber drivers. In 2026: ~200,000+ formal ride-hail vehicles,
and millions of informal taxi and minibus trips daily.
This fleet did not exist 10 years ago. It is not yet monetized as media.

**3. Android has commoditized the delivery hardware.**
A 4G Android tablet capable of video playback and GPS logging now costs $80–100.
The infrastructure cost to deploy a screen in every vehicle is at its lowest point in history.
The window for infrastructure ownership is open — but won't stay open.

## Why Emerging Markets Accelerate This Model

**1. Smartphone + cheap data has leapfrogged desktop.**
Nigeria has 225M people, ~130M smartphone users, and 4G LTE in major cities.
The mobile advertising market is growing 25%+ per year.
In-vehicle screens reach audiences where mobile attention is already dominant.

**2. Traffic density creates forced attention.**
Lagos traffic: average commuter sits in traffic 3+ hours/day.
A captive passenger in a ride-hail vehicle with a screen in front of them
has nowhere else to look. Attention quality is structurally better than any feed.

**3. No incumbents have built this.**
DStv (dominant pay TV), outdoor billboard companies, and radio stations
dominate Nigerian advertising. None have built a verified in-vehicle network.
There is no Uber Ads product in Nigeria. The category is open.

**4. Formal economy formalizing.**
Nigeria's ride-hail sector, once dominated by informal taxis, is rapidly formalizing.
Uber, Bolt, InDriver, ChopDrop, and local operators are creating organized,
trackable fleets — exactly the supply base we need.

**5. Advertiser sophistication increasing.**
Nigerian brands — Dangote, MTN, Access Bank, Guinness — are increasingly
demanding digital accountability. CPMs with no verification are losing.
Out-door's verified model arrives at exactly the moment advertisers demand proof.

---

## Market Timing Risk

**Too early:** Advertisers not yet demanding in-vehicle. (Low — major brands already asking for alternatives to meta/outdoor.)

**Too late:** Large platforms (Uber, Bolt) build their own. (Moderate risk — but their ad products are in-app, not in-vehicle physical screens. Different product.)

**Window:** 2025–2027 is the optimal window. After that, category will be more crowded. Before 2025, the device cost and connectivity weren't ready.
